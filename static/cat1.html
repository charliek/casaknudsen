RESTAURANTS
There are many fine restaurants in easy and leisurely walking distance (especially important for the walk home) from five star italian restaurants to 10 peso quesadillas stands to dance 'til dawn clubs. The fun part is how many cultural cuisines are available besides good mexican food. We really like the Mayan food the best as it is a real interesting mix of spice, fruit and seasoned meats, and the Mexican and Italian are great too. Even Thai and French ...
SHOPPING
For all of your staples for a week, the Walmart in town is a $3 taxi ride, one trip does it for a week. The grocery store is pretty comprehensive.

The main walking street (Fifth Avenue) has many shops, from specialty jewelry and upscale items to a variety of mexican artisans selling leather, pottery, glass, clothing and art work. There are also a number of galleries throughout the city including our favorite where we bought most of the artwork in the unit selling exclusively the Mexican artist De Anda.
NIGHT LIFE
Fifth avenue which is a block away features dozens of nice bars and places to enjoy a cool beverage, most close around 2am. For the stout of heart (and young of body) there are also many roof top and dance clubs with in walking distance which will stay open until 5 or 6am (makes for a laid back beach day after the sun comes up...).
